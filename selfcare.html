Self Care

What is self care?

Self-care involves intentional actions people take to preserve their physical, mental, and emotional health. Emphasize that it is not selfish but essential for well-being.

Stress Management System

Deep Breathing

Teach visitors simple breathing exercises (e.g., box breathing, 4-7-8 technique) that can be done anywhere to reduce stress and anxiety.

Progressive Musculer Relaxation

Guide users through this technique, which involves tensing and relaxing different muscle groups to release physical tension and promote relaxation.

Mindfullness and Meditation

Mindfullness Practice

Mindfulness as being fully present in the moment, and offer tips on how to practice it in daily life (e.g., mindful eating, mindful walking).

Guided Meditation

Provide links to free or affordable guided meditation sessions or create your own audio/visual content to help users engage in meditation.

Benifits Of Meditation

Highlight research showing how meditation can help reduce anxiety, improve focus, and increase self-awareness.

Positive Affrimation Journiling And Hobbies

Affirmation

Encourage users to adopt positive affirmations (e.g., "I am capable," "I deserve happiness") to reframe negative thoughts and improve self-esteem.

Journiling

o "What are three things I am grateful for today?" o "How do I feel today and why?"

Trying New Activities

Trying something new (e.g., learning a language, cooking, or a craft) to build confidence, engage the brain, and reduce monotony.

Social Communication And Gratitude practice

Building New Support Network

Stress the importance of maintaining relationships with family, friends, or support groups. Explain that meaningful connections can reduce loneliness and provide emotional support during tough times.

Communicating Needs

Provide tips on how to communicate mental health needs effectively to loved ones, whether it’s asking for help or setting boundaries.

Simple Gratitude Exercise

: Offer easy practices like saying "thank you" more often, reflecting on positive moments at the end of the day, or sharing things they appreciate with others.

Self-Compassion

Being Kind To Yourself

Teach the importance of self-compassion—treating yourself with the same kindness and understanding as you would a close friend.

Recognize Self-Critical Thought

Help users identify and challenge their negative self-talk with gentle, compassionate thoughts.

Self Acceptence

Provide tools to help people accept their imperfections and embrace themselves as they are, focusing on growth rather than perfection.
