Therapy and Treatment

Psychotherapy (Talk Therapy)

Coginitive Behaviour Therapy

Targets negative thought patterns and behaviors to treat depression, anxiety, PTSD, and OCD. Helps patients reframe thoughts and develop healthier coping strategies.

Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT)

Specialized for intense emotions (e.g., BPD, self-harm). Focuses on mindfulness, emotional regulation, distress tolerance, and interpersonal skills.

Psychodynamic Therapy

Examines how unconscious thoughts and early life experiences affect present behavior, offering insight for conditions like depression and anxiety.

Humanistic Therapy

Emphasizes personal growth and self-acceptance in a non-judgmental setting. Commonly includes client-centered or Gestalt therapy.

Exposure Therapy

Treats phobias, PTSD, and OCD by gradually exposing patients to feared situations in a controlled way to reduce anxiety.

Medication

Antidepressant

Used for depression and anxiety; includes SSRIs (e.g., Prozac), SNRIs, and tricyclics. Alters neurotransmitters to improve mood; potential side effects include nausea and insomnia.

Anti-Anxiety Medications

Includes benzodiazepines (e.g., Xanax) and buspirone. Calms the nervous system but may be addictive; requires careful management.

Mood Stabilizers

Manages bipolar disorder by balancing extreme mood swings. Examples include lithium and valproate.

Antipsychotics

Treats schizophrenia and bipolar disorder by regulating brain chemicals to reduce delusions, hallucinations, and mood swings.

Alternative and Complementary Therapies

Art Therapy

Uses creative expression to help process emotions, especially beneficial for children and trauma survivors.

Music Therapy

Incorporates music to reduce anxiety, improve mood, and facilitate emotional expression.

Animal-Assisted Therapy

Involves animals to reduce stress and enhance emotional well-being, helpful for PTSD and depression.

Yoga and MBSR

Yoga and mindfulness practices reduce anxiety and improve focus by promoting inner peace.

Acupuncture

An ancient practice that may reduce anxiety and depression by stimulating the body’s energy flow.

Group Therapy

Involves shared sessions for mutual support and skill-building, beneficial for addiction, social anxiety, and emotional regulation.
• Types of Group Therapy: o Support Groups: Focus on mutual support (e.g., AA for addiction). o Skills-Based Groups: Teach coping skills and tools, such as DBT skills groups for managing emotions.

Family and Couples Therapy

Focuses on improving communication, conflict resolution, and support systems within families and relationships.
: Discuss how it can be helpful for families dealing with a child’s mental illness or for couples struggling with relationship issues due to stress or trauma.
